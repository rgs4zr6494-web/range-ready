Twice-Daily Baseline — Exercises 7, 2, 6, 17, 22

Perform in order. 2 sets each. ~30 seconds per set. Rest 15 sec between sets.

#7
Pelvic Thrust
Floor Models
2×12
▼
Stand on floor model in your normal stance. Drive your hips forward in a controlled pelvic thrust motion — slow and deliberate. Feel the pelvic floor activate and the low back decompress. This resets the base of your spine before anything else loads it.
Pelvic Floor Activation Low Back Tension Release Hip Range of Motion
Farm work note: 30 years of bouncing and compression loads the lumbar spine downward. This exercise directly reverses that. Do not skip it — it is why Dr. Joe leads the baseline with it.
#2
Multi-Plane Squat
Floor Models
2×10
▼
Slow squat on floor models moving through multiple planes — forward, lateral, rotational. Let the discs allow natural movement. Feel the ankle, knee, and hip chain activate together. This is the foundational lower body exercise the entire system builds on.
Ankle Mobility Knee Stability Spine Stability
Golf: this is the ankle-to-hip chain your weight transfer needs. Archery: it builds the stable base your draw arm works against. Both sports live here.
#6
Full Body Rotation w/ Dowel
Floor Models + Club or Dowel
2×10 each
▼
Hold a club or dowel across your shoulders. Stand on floor models in golf stance. Rotate fully in both directions — slow, deliberate, end-range holds 3 seconds. Feel where the hip freedom wants to take the rotation. This is your slow-motion swing pattern drill built into the baseline.
Torso Mobility Hip Mobility Ankle Mobility Low Back Stability
The ribcage opener lives here. This is also the treestand archery shot pattern — both sports training simultaneously in one exercise.
#17
Shoulder ROM — Serratus Anterior
Handheld
2×10 each
▼
Handheld in one hand. Slow overhead reach with resistance — take the shoulder through its full elevation range. Work both arms. At end range hold 3–5 seconds and breathe. This directly addresses the shoulder chain that 30 years of archery has loaded asymmetrically.
General Shoulder Mobility Shoulder Elevation Frozen Shoulder Reduction Rotator Cuff
Archery: this is the single most important baseline exercise for draw-side shoulder health. The serratus anterior is the muscle that keeps the shoulder blade from winging during a held draw.
#22
Lat Release
Handheld
2×8 each
▼
Handheld. Cross-body pull and hold — reach across and down, feel the lat and the entire shoulder-to-hip fascial chain release. Hold 5 seconds at end range. This is the bookend exercise that closes the session — releasing everything that was activated and integrated above.
Low Back Tension Release Shoulder Joint Mobility Torso Mobility Hip Mobility
Forearm note: tight lats are a hidden driver of forearm and wrist tension. This exercise relieves the lat-forearm fascial chain indirectly. Often the wrist feels better after this than after any direct wrist work.
